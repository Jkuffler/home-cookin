Slow-cooked Pork Shoulder

Description

Makes great sliders & street tacos!

Ingredients

  • 2.5 lbs Bone-in Pork Shoulder (larger layer of fat yields greater chance for a crispy bite and stronger flavor)
  • 1 Fresh Shallot
  • 1 Fresh Garlic Bulb
  • Preferred All-Spice/Seasoning Mix
  • Olive Oil
  • Vinegar
  • Preferred BBQ sauce/mix

Steps

  1. preheat a lightly oiled cast-iron skillet using mid heat
  2. preheat slow cooker on med/high heat with 1 tbsp oil and vinegar to taste
  3. peel, dice, and simmer shallot
  4. peel and simmer all cloves of garlic from the bulb
  5. carefully score the bone/membrane portion of pork and toss out
  6. brush a thin layer of oil on pork shoulder
  7. optional: score pork and then stuff fresh or simmered garlic/shallot into the meat
  8. apply a seasoning rub to pork shoulder
  9. sear pork shoulder fat-side down and turning it until evenly sear
  10. after searing add the pork shoulder to slow cooker at desired temp
  11. remove bones after enough time has passed - about 3 hours using medium heat
  12. after about 6 hours meat should be easily shredded with a wooden spoon, salad fork, or tongs and all garlic & shallot should be dissolved in pork fat
  13. let meat cool in separate storage container and refrigerate (once cooled) for a minimum 10-12 hours
  14. using ~1/2 tbsp olive oil: reheat (to desired heat) pork in the cast iron pan/skillet to achieve crispy bite
  15. makes a great protein choice for tacos, sliders, or part of a balanced breakfast
