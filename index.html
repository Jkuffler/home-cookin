Chocolate Chip Cookies

Planning to share? These freshly baked, from-scratch cookies will make you super popular!

Ingredients

  • 2.5 sticks room-temp unsalted butter
  • 1.25 cups granulated sugar
  • .75 cups brown sugar
  • 2 room-temp eggs
  • Vanilla extract – to taste
  • 2.25 cups flour
  • 1–1.5 tsp baking soda
  • Sea salt – to taste
  • 2 cups chocolate chips

I like my cookies thin & crisp

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F
  2. Lightly mix butter and sugars
  3. Add eggs one at a time
  4. Add vanilla
  5. Mix dry ingredients separately
  6. Combine dry into wet
  7. Fold in chocolate chips
  8. Bake ~11 minutes total
  9. Cool and enjoy

Consuming raw or uncooked ingredients may be harmful to your health

Slow-cooked Pork Shoulder

Description

Makes great BBQ sliders & street tacos!

Ingredients

  • 2.5 lbs Bone-in Pork Shoulder (larger layer of fat yields greater chance for a crispy bite and stronger flavor)
  • 1 Fresh Shallot
  • 1 Fresh Garlic Bulb
  • Preferred All-Spice/Seasoning Mix
  • Olive Oil
  • Vinegar
  • Preferred BBQ sauce/mix

Steps

  1. preheat a lightly oiled cast-iron skillet using mid heat
  2. preheat slow cooker on med/high heat with 1 tbsp oil and vinegar to taste
  3. peel, dice, and simmer shallot
  4. peel and simmer all cloves of garlic from the bulb
  5. carefully score the bone/membrane portion of pork and toss out
  6. brush a thin layer of oil on pork shoulder
  7. optional: score pork and then stuff fresh or simmered garlic/shallot into the meat
  8. add the remaining simmered shallot and garlic to slow cooker
  9. apply a generous seasoning rub to pork shoulder
  10. sear pork shoulder fat-side down turning it occasionally until evenly seared
  11. after searing add the shoulder to slow cooker at med/med-high temp
  12. begin to remove bones after enough time has passed - about 3 hours using medium heat
  13. after about 6 hours meat should be easily shredded with a wooden spoon, salad fork, or tongs and all garlic & shallot should be dissolved in pork fat
  14. let meat cool in separate storage container and (once safely cooled) refrigerate for a minimum 10-12 hours
  15. using ~1/2 tbsp olive oil: reheat (to desired heat) pork in the cast iron pan/skillet to achieve crispy bite
  16. makes a great protein choice for tacos, sliders, or part of a balanced breakfast

French Toast

Ingredients

  • 5-6 slices sourdough: ideally set out to dry for a few hours
  • 1.5 cup creamy oat milk: room temperature
  • 2 eggs: room temperature
  • ground cinnamon
  • vanilla extract
  • desired fruits
  • half stick of preferred butter: room temp
  • syrup or honey
  • optional: oatmeal
  • powdered sugar
  • cinnamon sugar

Steps

  1. preheat skillet to medium
  2. for juicier fruit: wash & cut fruit then heat at 400-450 F in the oven while cooking french toast
  3. add vanilla to milk: to taste or color preference
  4. carefully whisk in one egg at a time into milk
  5. whisk thoroughly egg whites and yolk: looking for a smooth, thick, wet and creamy batter (small air bubbles are fine as long as your aren't heating the batter by over-whisking)
  6. sprinkle desired cinnamon onto batter - mix if you like or let it sit atop batter
  7. at the skillet: raise heat slightly and apply a chip of butter per piece of bread
  8. gently place bread onto batter, flip
  9. if needed: gently remove excess batter back to container
  10. for evenly cooked french toast: cook each side until brown - adding light butter when flipping to uncooked side
  11. optional: sprinkle a layer of oatmeal onto wet french toast and cook into bread for an extra crunch
  12. turn off skillet or pan
  13. save extra batter for no more than 24hrs
  14. add fruit - fresh or baked
  15. add maple syrup or honey to taste
  16. sprinkle cinnamon sugar and/or powdered sugar - serve and enjoy
